About

I work across both thermal and non-thermal plasma systems, focusing on plasma diagnostics, laser–plasma interaction studies, and plasma-based environmental applications. My background includes optical emission spectroscopy, electron temperature modeling, and the use of high-voltage plasma sources for chemical analysis and reaction studies.

Currently, I am developing advanced diagnostic approaches for measuring OH radical reactivity and performing time-resolved plasma analysis. This work involves synchronizing picosecond laser pulses and Nd:YAG laser systems with plasma sources to probe fast reactive species and transient plasma-chemical processes with high temporal precision.

My broader research goal is to build reliable, high-resolution diagnostic platforms that reveal the underlying mechanisms of plasma-based reactions and support the development of plasma technologies for environmental and scientific applications.
